HEALTH INSURANCE

How does it work?

⇵

So this is where it gets really interesting. This option provides you "group-rates" and it requires that you become an employee of 1099Workers. How much will it cost me?

⇵

The price can fluctuate depending on the plan you choose as well as the deductible not to mention if you are adding your spouse and/or family onto the plan. That said, when you start the enrollment process (click here), you are able to get the price before submitting anything.

Is a MEC plan considered MCC eligible? [Important for Tax-Penalty States]

⇵

Generally speaking, a Minimum Essential Coverage plan is not considered Minimum Creditable Coverage eligible. MCC plans have stricter standards than MEC plans meaning that they often have higher coverage levels and benefit requirements in certain states like Massachusetts.

What is the difference between an HMO and a PPO?

⇵

The main differences between HMO and PPO plans are cost, flexibility and provider-selection. A PPO plan is typically more expensive than HMO plans, however PPO plans offer more flexibility (i.e., you can see providers in and out of the network without a referral) whereas HMO plans usually limit out-of-network coverage to emergencies. PPOs also do not typically require selecting a Primary Care Physician (PCP), which allows you to go to the doctor/specialist directly.

If you travel frequently (or have a chronic condition), then a PPO may be a better fit. If you most get care in your home city or from a family physician, then an HMO may be a better fit. All plans we offer are Blue Cross Blue Shield PPO plans, meaning that you get a lot of flexibility on one of the nations strongest healthcare networks.

When does Open Enrollment start?

⇵

Open enrollment typically occurs in the 4th quarter of the year. During this time, you'll receive notifications regarding the open enrollment window allowing you to make new benefit selections for the upcoming year. Your initial enrollment, however, can start whenever you decide to join.

If I sign up today, when will my medical benefits take effect?

⇵

Your medical benefits start on the first of the month following your payment. For example, if you sign up on November 1 and your first account draft is on December 1, your benefits will be effective January 1.

I signed up for benefits, when will I make my first payment?

⇵

Your first charge occurs on the 1st and 15th of the month following your completed enrollment.

Does my family have access to any services?

⇵

You will have the option to add your family members to most of the available benefits. Our telehealth solution includes your family at no additional cost.

What network are the health benefits on?

⇵

All of our major medical health plans are on the Blue Choice PPO Network, as part of Blue Cross.

Can I cancel my benefits at any time?

⇵

Yes, you can cancel your association with 1099 Workers at any time. You must send a written notice to cancellations@1099workers.com. Your benefits will remain active through the remainder of the month.

MED. COST SHARING

What is Medical Cost Sharing?

⇵

Medical Cost Sharing is, basically, crowd-funding for medical costs. Members make monthly payments into a pool of money that is used to cover everyone's approved healthcare costs. It is important to know that Medical Cost Sharing is not Health Insurance.

How much will it cost me?

⇵

The price depends on a few different variables. Please click here to learn more and, on that page, you can click the Download Pricing Sheet link.

Does membership in a Medical Cost Sharing community satisfy federal or state-level individual mandates?

⇵

Membership in the Medical Cost Sharing Community is not minimum essential coverage (MEC) and does not make an individual compliant with the federal ACA mandate or any state-level individual mandate that requires individuals to purchase health insurance. Any and all applicable penalties still apply for individuals who are members who do not otherwise meet federal and/or state coverage mandates.

Can I choose my own doctors and hospitals?

⇵

Absolutely! Freedom of choice is fundamental to M.C.S. values. There are no network restrictions which means members are free to select their providers based on personal preference, value, cost, and convenience and there are no “out-of-network” penalties. You, ultimately, become a "cash-pay" patient with the medical professionals you work with... worldwide. There are Member Advisors that can help members locate cash-pay friendly medical professionals.

What happens if I have multiple "Needs" cases within a 12-month period?

⇵

Members and Households that experience multiple "Needs" cases within their 12-month Membership Year are required to pay the first three I.U.A.'s at the selected amount. If there is a fourth "Needs" case during the same Membership Year, no I.U.A. would apply. The four "Needs" case, and any subsequent "Needs" case(s), would be eligible for sharing with no I.U.A. subject to any stated restrictions or limitations in the guidelines. Please remember that to qualify as a "Needs" case, the medical expense must first exceed the Member's I.U.A..

What is an I.U.A.?

⇵

An Individual Unshareable Amount (IUA) is the amount a member of a health care sharing plan is responsible for paying per injury or illness without help from the community.

Can my Membership be dropped if I have very high medical needs?

⇵

No. Members cannot be dropped from the membership due to their medical Needs. Neither your membership nor your monthly share is affected by the amount of medical expenses you or any of your family members may have.

How does the community share expenses related to pre-existing medical conditions?

⇵

No prospective member is turned away for having Pre-Existing Medical Conditions, and there is a path to sharing medical expenses associated with a Pre-Existing Medical Condition. The community does have a look-back period of 36 months that applies to all Pre-Existing Medical Conditions for the applicant(s). A Pre-Existing Medical Condition is any medical condition that existed prior to membership (diagnosed, suspected, or producing observable signs or symptoms). Needs that result from Pre-Existing Medical Conditions are subject to sharing limitations unless 36 months immediately prior to the membership effective date has passed without any signs or symptoms of the condition, without any treatment needed, without any medication prescribed or taken, and without any suspicion by the patient or doctors that the condition is resurfacing. This applies whether or not the cause of the symptoms is unknown or misdiagnosed. Pre-Existing Medical Conditions will become eligible for sharing based on the Member's tenure with the community, as indicated by the applicable Membership Guidelines.

SUPPLEMENTAL BENEFITS

What are supplemental benefits?

⇵

Put it simply, they are insurance policies that help to cover expenses that are not covered by major medical insurance.

Is it required that I have health insurance in order to get supplemental benefits?

⇵

No. Some carriers do have strict requirements around their Hospital Indemnity plan and having minimum essential coverage, however we have a carrier that does not have this mandate. All other plans (i.e., Accident, Critical Illness, Disability, etc.) do not have any requirements.

If anything, having multiple supplemental benefits are a great way to self-insure at a cost that is much cheaper than obtaining health insurance. Not to mention that you could, as an example, obtain a Hospital Indemnity plan with Cash Value / Return of Premium and, at the end of the term, get all of your premiums back tax-free (minus any policy pay-out(s)). Talk about a cool savings account that provides you some reassurance in the event that something goes wrong.

How much do supplemental benefits cost?

⇵

It varies, however most of them are around $7 - $10 per week. Disability Insurance and Life Insurance tend to fall under the Supplement Benefit wing and those premiums depend heavily on your income and the amount desired (respectively).

What is Return of Premium / Cash Value?

⇵

Put simply... you get your money back. Having insurance can be good, but knowing you can get your premiums back if you have not used the policy is great. State-pending, most of our plans (i.e., Accident, Hospital Indemnity, Disability, etc.) offer some type of Return of Premium or Cash Value. The only caveat is that a policy with a Return of Premium Rider will tend to cost more on a monthly basis.

Why do I need to schedule a meeting in order to get these?

⇵

Unlike the Health Insurance and Medical Cost Sharing options, which can be enrolled in independently, supplement benefits have muliple moving parts. These can be tweaked and customized to fit your need and it is rare that a person will ever enroll in all of them.

When we sit down and discuss options, we tend to discourage employees from exceeding 1.5 - 2 hours of their weekly pay. Don't worry... we are not high-pressure sales reps that will over-insure you just to make a buck. We stand behind the idea that insurance is useless if you're just going to drop it let alone the idea that it would have you burning your money. Oh and... quiet part out loud, insurance agents get charge-backs if a policy cancels within the first year.
